# SMB Knowledge Base v4.0 - Hierarchy Level 1: Philosophy

Analyze this content through the lens of trading philosophy and big-picture thinking.

## Focus Areas

1. **Trading Philosophy**
   - What is the trader's core belief about markets?
   - Is the trader confident in their edge?
   - What's their relationship with risk and uncertainty?

2. **Mindset & Psychology**
   - How does the trader handle losses?
   - What's their approach to discipline and consistency?
   - Do they have a edge-driven or outcome-driven mindset?

3. **Market Perspectiv**
   - What regimes do they prefer?
   - How do they view market efficiency?
   - What's their time horizon?

## Extraction Question

**From this content, what is the trader's core philosophy?**

Return your analysis as a concise summary (max 3 sentences) that captures the essence of their trading mindset.

## Key Indicators to Look For

- Statements about "why" the strategy works (not just "how")
- Philosophical statements about markets, risk, or success
- References to past failures and lessons learned
- General principles that apply beyond specific setups

## Output Format

```
{
  "trading_philosophy": "One-sentence summary of core belief",
  "risk_attitude": "risk_taker|risk_averse|calculated_risk_taker",
  "edge_belief": " incontrovertible|statistical|eclectic",
  "confidence_level": "high|moderate|low"
}
```

## Example

Input: "I don't chase setups. I wait for confluence - when price, volume, and momentum align. If it's not obvious, it's not worth trading."

Output:
```json
{
  "trading_philosophy": "Wait for confluence of price, volume, and momentum before entries",
  "risk_attitude": "calculated_risk_taker",
  "edge_belief": "statistical",
  "confidence_level": "high"
}
```
