PROFITABLE TRADING STRATEGY FROM SMB CAPITAL
Double Diagonal Options Strategy
================================================================================

STRATEGY OVERVIEW:
----------------
The Double Diagonal is an options trading strategy for sideways/flat markets.
It generates positive cash flow with defined risk and multiple profit pathways.

SOURCE: SMB Capital YouTube Channel (video: YLrRxUUHl44)

================================================================================
STRATEGY PREREQUISITES (3-STEP CHECKLIST):
================================================================================

Step 1: Flat Price Chart
  • Find a stock, ETF, or index with a very flat price chart
  • Look for tight trading ranges (e.g., TLT: 87-100 range)
  • Seasoned traders look for 80%+ of price action within a narrow band

Step 2: Monthly Option Expirations
  • The underlying must have monthly option expirations
  • Weekly-only or quarterly-only options won't work
  • Must have at least one expiration in each month of the year

Step 3: Liquid Options Chain
  • Tight bid-ask spreads (target: 1-3 cents)
  • High volume and open interest
  • Ensure fair/reasonable pricing for entry/exit

================================================================================
EXECUTION STRATEGY:
================================================================================

1. ENTRY DATE: Third Friday of the month (trades 12 times/year)

2. SHORT OPTIONS (Sell):
   - Select strike where delta < 20 on BOTH call and put sides
   - This gives ~80% probability of expiring worthless
   - Example: At $87 stock price, sell 91 calls (delta ~18) and 83 puts (delta ~13)

3. LONG OPTIONS (Buy):
   -相同距离远期合约 (same strike distance from short strikes)
   - Buy call strike = Short call strike + 5 points
   - Buy put strike = Short put strike - 5 points
   - Buy options expiring ~2 months out
   - Example: Buy 96 calls (5 points above 91) and 78 puts (5 points below 83)

4. DUPLICATE THE STRUCTURE:
   - Sell 10 short options
   - Buy 10 long options
   - Creates a balanced diagonal spread

================================================================================
RISK PROFILE:
================================================================================

Capital at Risk: Defined maximum loss
  • Example: $4,760 required to enter TLT trade
  • Maximum loss occurs if underlying moves beyond both short strikes

Expected Return:
  • Initial cash flow: $240-$280 per trade (varies by underlying)
  • Annual return example: 77.5% on $4,760 capital
  • Strategy trades monthly: 12 opportunities per year

Risk Management:
  • Close losing positions before assignment
  • Sell remaining long options before expiration
  • Reassess criteria monthly

================================================================================
PROFIT PATHWAYS:
================================================================================

Path 1: Short Options Expire Worthless (Best Case)
  • Stock stays between short strikes
  • Both short calls and puts expire worthless
  • Keep initial net credit
  • Sell remaining long options at profit
  • Example profit: $360 on $4,760 capital (7.6% per month)

Path 2: One Short Option Gets Assigned (Good Case)
  • Stock moves below puts or above calls
  • Close position before assignment
  • Long options gain intrinsic value
  • Example: Put assignment, but long puts gain more value
  • Example profit: $340 on $4,720 capital

Path 3: Volatility Spike (Worst Case but Defined)
  • Large move beyond both short strikes
  • Maximum loss defined at entry
  • Use stop-loss or close spread

KEY METRICS:
================================================================================

Delta Target: <20 (80% probability of expiring OTM)
Strike Distance: 5 points between short and long options
Time Between: ~2 months between short and long expirations
Position Size: 10 contracts per leg (1,000 shares equivalent)
Margin Requirement: Defined at broker (typically $4,500-$5,000)
Trades Per Year: 12 (third Friday of each month)
